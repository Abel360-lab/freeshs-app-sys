Contract Issuance & Orientation

Comprehensive orientation on end-to-end delivery processes and contractual obligations

1

Delivery Process Orientation

  • Deliver food items per agreed quantity, quality, weight, and specified time period
  • Mandatory stamping of all delivered items with company name and authorized initials
  • Share Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA) with GCX prior to delivery commencement
  • Maintain continuous communication throughout the delivery process
2

Delivery Verification Standards

GCX Monitoring and Assessment Officers will conduct comprehensive checks:
  • Expiry Dates: Minimum 1 year to expiry or best before date
  • Weight Verification: Random sampling of reasonable size
  • Agreed Measurements: Verification of Kg/Ltr specifications
  • Quantity Validation: As per Purchase Order specifications
3

Documentation Requirements

Ensure proper authorization and documentation at distribution centres:
  • GCX Waybills must be signed and stamped by authorized personnel
  • Store Receipt Vouchers (SRV) require signatures from:
    • Storekeeper AND Head of School or Bursar
    • GCX Monitoring Officers (if present on-site)
  • All documents must bear official school stamps

Contract Processing Steps

  • Soft copy of contract sent to sub-supplier for thorough review
  • GCX tables food items, PPA-guided prices, and quantities
  • Formal contract issued with detailed obligations for both parties
  • Grading parameters provided for grain quality standards
  • Distribution schedule drafted for assigned schools
  • Contract signed by authorized representatives of both parties
3

Purchase Order Issuance

Order processing and distribution of essential supplier resources

1

Purchase Order Creation

Based on contractual agreements, GCX issues detailed Purchase Orders containing:
  • Specific items and quantities to be supplied
  • Delivery commencement dates
  • Completion timelines per distribution schedule
  • Regional pricing variations (logistics considerations)
2

Approval Process

All Purchase Orders undergo formal approval by the Head of Entity or designated delegate before issuance to ensure compliance and accuracy.

Invoicing & Payment Process

Documentation submission and payment processing procedures

1

Required Invoice Documentation

Submit complete documentation package immediately after delivery:
  • Original stamped and signed School SRVs
  • Completed GCX Waybills with all signatures
  • Sub-Supplier invoice on official company letterhead
  • All documents must be originals (no photocopies)
2

Payment Processing

  • GCX forwards invoices to Ministry of Education in batches
  • Electronic payment processed upon receipt of funds from MoE
  • All payments made via bank transfer (no cash payments)
  • Payment timelines subject to MoE fund disbursement
3

Statement of Account

To request Statement of Account:
  • Submit official request on company letterhead
  • Address to GCX Finance Department
  • Must be signed by authorized company representative
  • Allow 3-5 business days for processing

Important Payment Information

Payment processing times may vary based on government funding cycles. Sub-suppliers are advised to maintain adequate working capital to ensure continuous supply capabilities. GCX is committed to expediting payments upon receipt of funds from the Ministry of Education.
